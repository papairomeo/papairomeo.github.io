The day you arrived

May 27, 2026

Son, today is May 27, 2026. I am thinking about you, as always. Today I decided to go back in time a little, to tell you about one of the most important days of my life: the day you were born.

Everything happened in Porto Alegre, in Rio Grande do Sul. It was the night of April 8, 2012. It was not just any night. It was the night the world was about to change for me, even though I did not yet know exactly how.

We went straight to Hospital de Clínicas, which was near home and where your mother had already been doing her prenatal appointments. When we arrived, the doctors quickly realized that your arrival was close and she was admitted. I rushed home to get some clothes she would need, and before midnight I was back at the hospital.

We spent the entire night there. It was a long and painful process. The contractions would come, ease up a little, then come back with full force. We tried everything to help. I remember warm baths, a pilates ball in the corridor, attempts to ease the pain as best we could. For a few hours, we even managed to sleep a little.

Around noon on April 9, you started showing signs that you were ready to arrive. Your mother was taken to the operating room, and I went in with her. I was nervous, worried, trying to be strong for both of us. I was there for every moment, watching the doctors work with care, and as frightening as it was, there was no other place in the world I wanted to be at that moment.

And then you were born.

Huge and beautiful. Healthy and perfect, weighing 3.385 kg (about 7.5 lbs).

Son, I don't have the right words to describe what I felt in that moment. It was the most incredible feeling of my life. It was when I understood what love means in a physical sense, because now I could touch you, see you, hold you in my arms. My eyes were full of tears and I had a smile I could not hold back. You were perfect, and everything had gone well.

We grew up together in those first two years, you and I. I took you to barbecues with friends, to work events where you were the star of the room. We walked through Parque da Redenção in Porto Alegre, full of trees and families. I made your bottle, changed your diapers, put you to sleep. And when you started taking your first steps, I was there, holding you, slowly letting go, cheering you on every time you stood up on your own.

You were always very curious. You loved crawling over to my computer and would stare at the screen with a funny look of determination, as if you knew exactly what you were doing. I let you. I was happy watching you explore the world in your own way.

When you were by my side, the difficulties out there seemed smaller. They didn't disappear, but they lost their strength. You were the best part of every day.

Then the distance came between us.

It was not something I chose, Romeo. And I won't tell you all the details right now, not because I want to hide something from you, but because some things need the right moment to be said, when you are ready to understand the complexity of adult life. What I can tell you is that being far from you was the hardest thing I have ever faced. Never, not for a single moment, did I stop thinking about you or wanting to be close.

Even from a distance, I tried to be present in the best way I could. When you went to New Zealand, we made an agreement so that you could come visit me every year. And in the years you came, I was the happiest father in the world.

You don't remember almost any of this, I know. You were very young. But I remember everything. Every detail. And that is why I am writing, so that if one day you want to know who your father is and where we came from together, the answers are here.

With love,
Papai
