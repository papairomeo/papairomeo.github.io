Letter 1 · Feeling

What is happening

May 19, 2026

Son, today is May 19, 2026. I'm in my room thinking about you. It's been 40 days since the last time we spoke, which was on your birthday. Before that, I had already gone nearly 2 months without talking to you, which had never happened before — not since you moved to New Zealand.

I don't know exactly how you are, and that feeling is painful. Maybe one day you'll understand, when you become a father. I made the decision to write to you so that you can get to know me better, should you ever decide that matters in your life.

I miss you deeply. I miss when you would reach out to have a call, to play Roblox and have fun together. Our Fridays and Saturdays are gone, and I don't know what made you decide to stop answering my messages or wanting to see me. That feeling is hard for me, because silence can be worse than a fight. In a fight, we usually know the reasons and causes behind the conflict. But being simply ignored — without anyone asking what's going on or wanting to hear the other side — is even harder for me.

I know adolescence is a confusing phase, that a lot changes inside us, and maybe you can't even properly explain what you feel. And that's okay. But I need to tell you: missing you hurts in a way that only love can explain.

I'm writing this not only for you, but for myself too — so that I can take some time to reflect on my feelings and understand that there isn't much I can do from a distance, especially when my messages are received but no longer answered the way they used to be.

Writing this little letter made me feel a little better. I think I'll keep doing this.

With love,
Papai
